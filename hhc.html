What is Homepathy?

What is the Holistic approach? Homeopathy medicine is characterized by the treatment of the whole person, taking into account mental, emotional and social factors, rather than just the symptoms of a disease.
For example, take a 60-year-old women suffering from asthma, vaginal dryness, obesity, and arthritic pains in her knees. In western medicine she will go to at least four different doctors and each will prescribe her at least four different, powerful, prescription drugs, with no one knowing or caring what the others prescribed for her. Whereas in Homeopathy she would be recommended to have some general blood tests performed to rule out hypothyroidism, tumors, and allergies. Then, we would go through an extensive homeopathic interview with questions about her physical, emotional, intellectual and sexual health. Based on the physical examination and the lab results, we would create an individualized prescription which is unique to this particular patient and would help tackle all four illnesses. Putting all this information together into one plan for each patient is a key component of the holistic approach to health care.
